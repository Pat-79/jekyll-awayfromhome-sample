Meridia Prime is the capital of Solmere, a compact country on the silver delta where the Rovan River meets the Irien Sea. We picked it for a family city trip because distances are short, transit is reliable, and public parks are everywhere. In four days we visited museums, waterways, and a science district without ever feeling rushed.

Solmere is proud of practical design. Meridia's old core is mostly pedestrian, while family services are concentrated near transit hubs in the East Arc region. The city runs "quiet streets" every afternoon around schools and markets, making central walking safer and much calmer than many other capitals.

## Activities to do

- Visit the Tideworks Science Hall with interactive exhibits.
- Ride the circular tram through the Lantern District.
- Rent family bikes in Crescent Park.
- Take a short evening river cruise on the Rovan Loop.

## Do and don't in Meridia Prime

- Do pre-book museum slots for weekend mornings.
- Do use tram day-passes for flexible travel.
- Do pack light rain layers; weather can switch quickly.
- Don't feed birds in protected city gardens.
- Don't cross quiet streets outside marked points.
- Don't expect late dining in residential districts.

## Activity I did: sunrise bike ride in Crescent Park

Before breakfast I rented a city bike and rode the inner loop of Crescent Park while the park cafés were opening. Families were doing light exercise, and maintenance crews were watering flower terraces before the heat arrived. It was a calm start that showed how livable Meridia is beyond its tourist highlights.
