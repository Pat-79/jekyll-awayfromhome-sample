I boarded in Port Celen, the maritime heart of Orindar, for a week-long cruise through the Zelorian Sea. This was my first cruise holiday, and I expected a floating hotel experience. Instead, it felt like a moving cultural route, because every stop had its own rhythm: cliff villages, citrus docks, and small island workshops where rope-making is still done by hand.

The Zelorian Sea lies between Orindar and the Talven Coast, and sailors call it "the calm mirror" during summer months. That reputation is mostly true, though afternoon currents around the Nyris Reaches can still be strong. Orindari ports are efficient but ceremonial: departures are announced with short horn patterns linked to old harbor guild traditions.

## Activities to do

- Join a sunrise deck stretch with views over open water.
- Visit shell-stone chapels on the island of Breth.
- Taste smoked citrus fish in Maro Harbor.
- Book a guided scuba dive on the Nyris coral reef.

## Do and don't on this route

- Do carry a light jacket even in warm season winds.
- Do arrive early for tender-boat transfers.
- Do use reef-safe sunscreen when swimming.
- Don't feed seabirds from deck railings.
- Don't schedule every hour; sea routes can shift.
- Don't skip local harbor briefings.

## Activity I did: scuba dive over the Nyris reef

At Nyris Lagoon I joined a guided scuba dive over the outer coral shelf. We descended along a rope line into clear blue water, then drifted above layered reef gardens full of fan coral and bright reef fish. Our dive lead pointed out a shallow arch where sunlight cut through in bands, and that underwater light made the whole experience feel cinematic and calm at the same time.
