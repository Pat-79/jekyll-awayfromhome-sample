The Istrava Highlands lie in northern Delcor, along the spine of the Varnic Mountains on the edge of the Pale Meridian Continent. I chose this as a slow-travel holiday, not a checklist trip. The route was simple: three villages in ten days, each connected by narrow valley rail and old shepherd paths.

Delcor's highland culture is shaped by weather and stone. Houses in the Istrava region are built with thick slate walls and bright painted doors so homes stay visible during misty afternoons. Local meals are hearty and seasonal, and village squares double as weekly produce exchanges where travelers are welcome to browse and chat.

## Activities to do

- Walk the ridge trail between Dravon and Kelmere.
- Visit highland dairies in the Sorn Valley.
- Join a village storytelling night in Veya.
- Photograph dawn fog over the Varnic foothills.

## Do and don't in the Istrava Highlands

- Do wear waterproof boots year-round.
- Do check valley rail times early in the day.
- Do learn a few Delcori greetings before arrival.
- Don't play loud music on shared trails.
- Don't pick wild herbs in protected meadows.
- Don't underestimate sunset temperature drops.

## Activity I did: dawn photo walk in Veya

I woke before sunrise in Veya and joined a local guide for a quiet photo walk above the village terraces. We waited at a ridge bend until fog rolled through the lower valley and sunlight lit the slate rooftops. That half hour was one of the most peaceful travel moments I have had in years, and the photos still remind me why slow itineraries work so well.
