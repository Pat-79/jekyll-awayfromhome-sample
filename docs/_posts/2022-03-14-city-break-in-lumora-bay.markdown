Lumora Bay is the capital-port of Bayland, a compact country on the western edge of the Auric Continent. It feels like a city trip designed for people who love detail: tiled stairways, tiny bookstores, and night markets where every table glows with amber lanterns. I came for a short city break, but the place made me slow down after the first morning.

Bayland has a reputation for careful urban planning. Old neighborhoods in Lumora Bay are preserved as low-rise blocks, while modern districts stand farther inland in the region of Casker Plain. That gives the center a very walkable rhythm. You can cross three distinct quarters in one afternoon: the old quay, the spice arcade, and the hillside observatory district.

## Activities to do

- Walk the Sky Steps at sunrise for views over Maren Inlet.
- Join a twilight street-food tasting in the Brindle Market arcades.
- Take a ferry loop on the Inner Lume to see the waterfront murals.
- Visit the Museum of Tides for Bayland's coastal history.

## Do and don't in Lumora Bay

- Do carry small cash notes for market stalls.
- Do reserve evening restaurant tables early, especially on weekends.
- Do greet vendors before asking for photos.
- Don't block stair lanes while taking pictures.
- Don't assume all ferries run after midnight.
- Don't bargain aggressively in fixed-price food halls.

## Activity I did: lantern cooking class

I joined a rooftop cooking class in the old quay district and learned to make smoked citrus rice with sea-herb oil, a classic Bayland comfort meal. The chef explained how local traders once combined mountain herbs from Norveil Ridge with shellfish from Maren Inlet, and that story turned dinner into a history lesson. Cooking with six other travelers also made the city feel less anonymous.
