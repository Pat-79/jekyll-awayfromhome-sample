The Syrel Peaks rise above northern Thaloria like a frozen wall of silver. I booked this as a wintersports vacation, expecting good slopes and little else, but the region turned out to be wonderfully layered. Small alpine villages in the Rimefold Valley connect by lift, train, and snow coach, so you can ski all day without spending hours in transfer lines.

Thaloria is known for strict mountain safety culture. Avalanche education is visible everywhere, from hotel lobbies to café table cards. In the Syrel region, local communities still use carved weather poles to share wind shifts between villages. It is practical and beautiful, and it gives the whole destination a sense of shared responsibility.

## Activities to do

- Ski the long blue run from Velm Crest into Frostmere.
- Try a beginner-friendly snowboard clinic in Kiro Pass.
- Take a moonlit snowshoe hike through Pineglass Forest.
- Warm up in open-air thermal pools at Brunn Hollow.

## Do and don't in the Syrel Peaks

- Do rent avalanche beacons for off-piste tours.
- Do pre-book lift passes online.
- Do respect quiet hours in village lodges.
- Don't leave marked pistes in poor visibility.
- Don't dry gear on wooden balcony rails.
- Don't ignore mountain guide instructions.

## Activity I did: first snowboard lesson

I took my first snowboard lesson on a gentle practice slope in Kiro Pass. The instructor taught us how to fall safely before teaching turns, and that made a huge difference in confidence. By late afternoon I could link short turns and descend without panic, which felt like a personal breakthrough in a place that initially looked intimidating.
